Start Here: 3 Simple Methods

1) CPM Ads (Most Automatic)

Pros: counts impressions, no clicks needed; ideal for autosurf. Cons: low CPM ($0.5–$3 per 1k views).

  • Networks tolerant to mixed traffic: BidVertiser, PopAds, AdCash.
  • Create a minimal landing page with 2–3 ad units and send autosurf traffic there.

2) Shortlinks (Direct Mode)

Pros: higher payout per 1k if direct-redirect is allowed. Cons: rare; risk of rejection if auto-bypass.

  • Look for Direct Link feature; avoid captcha/interstitial flows for bots.

3) Video Rewards & Faucets

Pros: reliable payouts via points → PayPal/crypto. Cons: needs many tabs/devices.

  • Examples: HideoutTV, LootTV, Idle-Empire, FireFaucet (auto-claim).

Article: Easy Ways to Earn Money Online (That Scale with Volume)

Making money online is often about repeatable micro-earnings. One action may pay only $0.001–$0.01, but repeated a thousand times a day across multiple devices, it adds up. The key is choosing methods that are (1) safe, (2) mostly automatic, and (3) scalable.

Why CPM Ads Fit Automation

CPM networks pay per impression, not per click. That makes them ideal for passive traffic sources like autosurf or rotators. While CPM is low, impressions are trivial to generate at scale and do not require user interaction. Use a clean landing page, avoid heavy scripts, and keep content clear to pass manual reviews.

Shortlinks: Use Only Direct Redirect

Traditional shorteners show an interstitial page with a “Continue” button or captcha—bots stop there. If you find a direct-link option (no button/captcha), you can blend it with CPM ads. Otherwise, stick to CPM.

Supplement with Video Rewards & Faucets

Video reward platforms and auto-faucets provide predictable daily drip. Run them on spare devices or low-cost VPSs. The earnings are small per device, but consistent, and you can spread across multiple accounts (respecting TOS).

Risk Management

  • Never mix autosurf traffic with Google AdSense or strict networks.
  • Scale slowly; sudden spikes may trigger extra reviews.
  • Separate real-user pages from autosurf pages using different URLs.
